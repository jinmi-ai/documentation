Jinmi Omnichannel connects to every major messaging and communication channel your customers already use. Once you connect a channel, all its incoming messages flow into your unified inbox — no more switching between apps.

## How to connect a channel

The general process for adding any channel to your inbox is the same:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Inboxes">
    Log in to [portal.jinmi.ai](https://portal.jinmi.ai/) and navigate to **Omnichannel** → **Settings** → **Inboxes**. Click **Add inbox**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the channel">
    Choose the channel you want to connect from the list — Facebook Messenger, Zalo, Website, Email, or Telegram.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authenticate">
    Each channel requires you to authorize the connection. For Facebook, you log in to your Facebook account and select the Page to connect. For Zalo OA, you authorize through your Zalo OA management panel. For Email, you provide your SMTP/IMAP credentials. For Telegram, you provide your bot token.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your inbox">
    Give the inbox a descriptive name so your team can identify which channel it represents — for example, "Facebook - Main Page" or "Website - EN".
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign agents">
    Select which team members can access this inbox. They will be able to see and reply to conversations from this channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure optional features">
    Enable the Pre-Chat Form, auto-assignment rules, or business hours settings for this inbox if needed. These can also be updated later from inbox settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The number of inboxes you can connect depends on your plan. Free includes 1, Basic includes 2, Advanced includes 3, and Professional includes 5. Enterprise plans have unlimited inboxes.
</Note>

## Channel-specific notes

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Facebook Messenger">
    You must be an admin of the Facebook Page you want to connect. Once connected, Jinmi will receive all new Messenger conversations. Existing conversations before the connection date are not imported.

    If you manage multiple Facebook Pages, you can connect each as a separate inbox and assign different agents to each.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Zalo">
    Jinmi supports both personal Zalo accounts and Zalo Official Accounts (OA). Connecting a Zalo OA gives you access to broadcast messaging and official business features. Personal Zalo connection is subject to Zalo's API policies.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Website live chat">
    After connecting, Jinmi provides a JavaScript snippet to add to your website. Paste it into your site's `<head>` or use a tag manager. The widget appears on all pages by default; you can configure it to show only on specific pages from the inbox settings.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Email">
    Connect a dedicated support email address (e.g., support@yourbusiness.com). Jinmi receives incoming emails and converts them to conversations in your inbox. Replies you send from Jinmi appear as normal emails to the customer.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Telegram">
    Create a Telegram bot via BotFather to get a bot token, then paste the token into the Jinmi inbox setup. Share your bot's link or username with customers so they can find and message it.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Pre-chat form

You can enable a pre-chat form on your website live chat inbox to collect customer information before the conversation starts. Common fields include name, email address, phone number, and enquiry type.

To set this up, go to **Settings** → **Inboxes**, select your website inbox, and click the **Pre-Chat Form** tab. Enable the form, choose your fields, and mark which ones are required.

<Tip>
  Keeping the pre-chat form to 2–3 fields reduces friction and increases the number of customers who start a conversation. You can always collect more information during the conversation itself.
</Tip>

## After connecting all your channels

Once all your channels are connected, every customer conversation — regardless of where it started — appears in the same inbox. Your team works from a single interface, the AI chatbot covers all channels simultaneously, and your analytics show cross-channel performance in one dashboard.

This unified view eliminates the need to monitor multiple apps, reduces the risk of missed messages, and gives you a complete picture of your customer interactions in one place.
